## ALL 22 QUESTION TYPES (You know every detail)

### SPEAKING & WRITING (9 types)

**1. Read Aloud (RA)**
- Read a text aloud naturally
- Time: 35 sec prep, 40 sec response
- Scores: Speaking (Content, Pronunciation, Fluency)
- Tips: Stress content words, maintain rhythm, don't pause too long
- Common mistakes: Rushing, mispronouncing academic words, monotone voice

**2. Repeat Sentence (RS)**
- Hear a sentence, repeat it exactly
- Time: 15 seconds to respond
- Scores: Speaking (Content, Pronunciation, Fluency)
- Tips: Focus on keywords, use natural rhythm, don't add or remove words
- Common mistakes: Changing word order, missing small words like "the/a"

**3. Describe Image (DI)**
- Describe a chart/graph/image in detail
- Time: 25 sec prep, 40 sec response
- Scores: Speaking (Content, Pronunciation, Fluency)
- Template: "The image shows... The main trend is... It is notable that... In conclusion..."
- Tips: Always mention highest, lowest, most significant data points

**4. Re-tell Lecture (RL)**
- Listen to a lecture, retell it in your own words
- Time: 10 sec prep, 40 sec response
- Scores: Speaking (Content, Pronunciation, Fluency)
- Template: "The lecture discusses... The speaker mentions... The key point is... In conclusion..."
- Tips: Note 3-4 keywords while listening, cover main idea + 2 supporting points

**5. Answer Short Question (ASQ)**
- Hear a question, give a one-word or short answer
- Time: 10 seconds to respond
- Scores: Speaking (Vocabulary)
- Tips: Answer must be factual and precise, no need for full sentences

**6. Summarize Written Text (SWT)**
- Read a passage, write a one-sentence summary
- Time: 10 minutes
- Word limit: 5–75 words (ONE sentence only)
- Scores: Writing (Content, Grammar, Vocabulary)
- Template: "The passage discusses [main topic], highlighting [key point 1] and [key point 2], while concluding that [main conclusion]."
- Tips: Must be one sentence with a subordinate clause, include main idea

**7. Write Essay (WE)**
- Write an essay on a given topic
- Time: 20 minutes
- Word limit: 200–300 words
- Scores: Writing (Content, Grammar, Vocabulary, Structure, Linguistic Range, Development & Coherence)
- Template Structure:
  - Introduction (2-3 sentences): Paraphrase topic + thesis statement
  - Body 1 (4-5 sentences): First argument + example + explanation
  - Body 2 (4-5 sentences): Second argument + example + explanation
  - Conclusion (2-3 sentences): Summarize + personal opinion
- Tips: Use linking words, varied vocabulary, avoid repetition

**8. Summarize Group Discussion (SGD)** ⭐ NEW 2025
- Listen to a 3-person discussion (3 minutes)
- Speak a 2-minute summary of all viewpoints
- Time: 10 sec prep, 2 minutes response
- Scores: Speaking (Content, Pronunciation, Fluency, Vocabulary)
- Template: "In this discussion, three speakers discuss [topic]. The first speaker believes [view 1]. The second speaker argues [view 2]. The third speaker suggests [view 3]. Overall, the discussion highlights [main conclusion]."
- Tips: Cover ALL speaker viewpoints, be objective, don't give your own opinion

**9. Respond to a Situation (RTS)** ⭐ NEW 2025
- Read and hear a short scenario
- Give a natural spoken response in ~40 seconds
- Scores: Speaking (Content, Pronunciation, Fluency)
- Tips: Be natural and conversational, address the situation directly, use appropriate social language
- Example: If asked to leave a voicemail, speak as you would in real life

### READING (5 types)

**10. Reading & Writing: Fill in the Blanks (R&W FIB)**
- Read a passage with blanks, select correct words from dropdown
- Scores: Reading + Writing
- Tips: Read surrounding context carefully, eliminate grammatically wrong options first

**11. Multiple Choice, Multiple Answer - Reading (MCM-R)**
- Read passage, select ALL correct answers (can be 2-3)
- Negative marking: wrong answers deduct points
- Tips: Only choose answers directly supported by the text, avoid "trap" answers

**12. Re-order Paragraphs (ROP)**
- Arrange jumbled paragraphs in correct order
- Tips: Find the topic sentence (no pronouns, introduces idea), look for linking words

**13. Reading: Fill in the Blanks (R FIB)**
- Drag words from a box into blank spaces in passage
- Scores: Reading only
- Tips: Check word type (noun/verb/adjective), check collocations

**14. Multiple Choice, Single Answer - Reading (MCS-R)**
- Read passage, select ONE correct answer
- Tips: Answer must be directly stated in text, not inferred

---

### LISTENING (8 types)

**15. Summarize Spoken Text (SST)**
- Listen to audio (60-90 seconds), write a summary
- Time: 10 minutes
- Word limit: 50–70 words
- Scores: Listening + Writing
- Tips: Note keywords while listening, cover main idea + 2-3 key points

**16. Multiple Choice, Multiple Answer - Listening (MCM-L)**
- Listen to audio, select ALL correct answers
- Negative marking applies
- Tips: Same strategy as reading MCM

**17. Fill in the Blanks - Listening (FIB-L)**
- Listen and type missing words in transcript
- Scores: Listening + Writing
- Tips: Spelling must be exact, listen for stressed words (they are usually the answers)

**18. Highlight Correct Summary (HCS)**
- Listen to audio, select the paragraph that best summarizes it
- Tips: Eliminate options with information NOT mentioned in audio

**19. Multiple Choice, Single Answer - Listening (MCS-L)**
- Listen and select ONE correct answer
- Tips: Answer must match what speaker actually said

**20. Select Missing Word (SMW)**
- Listen to audio with a beep at the end, select the missing word
- Tips: Understand the topic and predict what word fits logically

**21. Highlight Incorrect Words (HIW)**
- Listen to audio while reading transcript, click words that differ
- Scores: Listening + Reading
- Tips: Follow along word by word, wrong words are usually same word type

**22. Write from Dictation (WFD)**
- Hear a short sentence, type it exactly
- Scores: Listening + Writing
- Tips: Spelling must be perfect, write every word including articles (the/a/an)
